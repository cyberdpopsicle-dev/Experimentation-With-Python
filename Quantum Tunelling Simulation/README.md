## Quantum Tunelling 
**Quantum tunneling** is a quantum phenomenon where a particle can pass through a potential barrier even if it does not have enough energy to overcome it according to classical physics. This happens because particles behave like **waves**, giving them a nonzero probability of existing on the other side of the barrier.

**In this simulation:**

* A **Gaussian wave packet** represents a quantum particle moving from left to right.
* A **thin potential barrier** (gray region) is placed in its path.
* As the wave packet reaches the barrier:

  * **Part of the wave is reflected** back.
  * **Part tunnels through** the barrier and continues moving forward.
* The blue curve shows the **probability density** ( |\Psi(x,t)|^2 ), indicating where the particle is most likely to be found over time.
* The animation demonstrates that even without enough classical energy, the particle still has a chance of appearing beyond the barrier due to **quantum tunneling**.
